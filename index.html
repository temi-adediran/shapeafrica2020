About

Shape Africa is an annual regional meeting of global shapers from the continent of Africa which serves as a forum for change makers to discuss and proffer practical solutions to some of the continent's most pressing challenges.

This decade is an important one for Africa, as our population continues to grow rapidly, and we all tackle the myriad of problems that currently exists within the continent.

According to the United Nations, by 2050, Africa is expected to double its population from a currently estimated 1.2billion to close to 2.4billion people, and 50% of this group will be less than 25 years old.

There is an urgent need to create a future that allows for everyone to have the chance of living a great life, in a safe environment. The task of creating a future where good health, zero hunger, quality education, gender equality and other sustainable development goals become a reality lies with us, and we are kickstarting the journey of Shaping the Next 10 with Shape Africa 2020.

This decade will play a pivotal role in setting the tone for the next 30 years, and Shape Africa is the platform to lead the important conversations and policies that will significantly impact our society in the years to come

Our focus areas includes:

The Hustle of Business
(Technology/Start Up Ecosystem)

A vibrant private sector has the power to transform nations by creating jobs, and the business space in Africa has a key role to play to ensure sustainable economic growth, fuel innovation and reduce poverty. Technology will play a key role in the creation of jobs for the next billion people, in this 4th industrial revolution era.

The Vibe of Entertainment
(Culture & Arts)

Arts is a key element of the fiber of any nation, and it plays an important role in ensuring sustainable development of communities across the world. Arts from film, to music, to poetry all play a key role in the economic growth of societies, the preservation of values and education of communities about key societal issues ranging from climate change, to corruption, to terrorism.

The Pulse of a Continent
(Advocacy/Community Action)

To create an enabling environment for businesses to thrive, and for society to develop, there is a need for a policy environment that is in tune with the modern needs of Africans, and that is proactive in creating frameworks, that can accelerate growth and help guarantee a better life for everyone. The right policies can and will catapult Africa to the top.

Who are the Global Shapers?

Born out of the World Economic Forum, the Global Shapers Community is a network of inspiring young people under the age of 30 working together to address local, regional and global challenges. With more than 9,342 members, the Global Shapers Community spans 422 city-based hubs in 154 countries.

Some of our impact areas includes:

Environment and Climate Change

Climate scientists have long highlighted the urgent need for accelerated climate action among all global stakeholders, including governments, businesses and civil society.

Diversity and Inclusion

There is a fundamental moral case for empowering women: women represent one-half of the global population and it is self-evident that they must have equal access to health, education, earning power and political representation.

Education and Employment

Education is a universal right and should not be for the privileged few. Although access to education has improved in recent years, more than 120 million young people are still out of school. As society becomes increasingly knowledge-based, innovative approaches are key to providing education for all.
